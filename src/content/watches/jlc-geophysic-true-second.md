A mechanical movement built, at real cost, to do what a battery does for free.

The dead-beat seconds complication — the Cal. 770's defining feature — makes the seconds hand tick in discrete one-second jumps rather than sweep continuously. A quartz watch does this automatically. JLC engineered a mechanism of considerable complexity to achieve the same result in a mechanical movement.

This is either absurd or admirable depending on your disposition. We think it is admirable.

Discontinued. Full set. The kind of watch that makes more sense the longer you think about it.
