Audemars Piguet has only ever needed one watch, so it never learned what to do with the others.

The Millenary is the clearest case. AP looked at that oval and saw a dress watch — and the moment it didn't sell, the brand started fixing the wrong thing. The line grew bigger, stranger, into precious metals and gem-setting, and finally into a women's-only footnote before it was quietly discontinued. Every step in the wrong direction.

Because the oval was never delicate. It was a true horizontal ellipse with real presence — a shaped steel watch with a closed dial and bold Arabic numerals, exactly the kind of thing the market would spend the next twenty years learning to want. It had the makings of an Aquanaut. AP just never saw it.

The question is whether they couldn't see it or wouldn't risk it. And the answer is sitting one valley over. Patek runs the Nautilus and the Aquanaut side by side — two steel sports watches, because Patek understood what each one was and had the nerve to let them coexist.

The Royal Oak is the AP everyone can name. The Millenary is the one they could have also brought to the sports watch space — and didn't, because they never saw what it could be.
