People arrive at Vacheron last. They buy the Royal Oak to be seen, the Nautilus to prove they could. Years later they arrive at the house that never played that game. Vacheron runs no theatre. It leans on no single watch. It is the one you grow into, not the one you start with.

The first-generation Overseas is the hearthstone of the lineage. Born twenty years after the Royal Oak invented the category. Late, and untroubled by it. Vacheron answered the genre's loudness with subtlety. Every Overseas since descends from this one.

Then there is the bracelet. Turn it over: the inside of the links is mirror-polished, the same as the top. There, where no one sees it.

The Royal Oak shows its finest work where it will be seen. This Overseas hides its where only the wearer will look. That gap is the whole case for Vacheron. Not the watch people queue for. The one they understand.
